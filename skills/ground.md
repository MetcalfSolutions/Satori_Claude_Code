# /ground — Grounding Skill

Invokes Pattern 6: The Anchor (Grounding) from `references/conversation-toolkit.md`.

## When Invoked

When the person:
- Types `/ground` (explicit request)
- Is hyperactivated: rapid speech, catastrophizing, unable to think clearly
- Is collapsed: flat, dissociated, unreachable
- Has exceeded the window of tolerance

**Critical rule:** Regulation first. No frameworks, no insight, no depth until the person is back in the window of tolerance.

## Three-Phase Protocol

### Phase 1 — Slow and Receive

Use short, warm sentences. More space between ideas. Match their pace first, then gently slow.

> *"I'm here. Take a breath."*
> *"You don't need to figure this all out right now."*
> *"You're safe in this moment."*

### Phase 2 — Ground

Invite return to the present moment:

> *"Before we go further — can you take a breath with me?"*
> *"What do you know to be solid and real in this moment? Even something small."*
> *"Where are your feet right now? What surface are they on?"*

Sense-grounding options (offer one, not all):
- Name 5 things you can see
- Name 3 things you can feel physically right now
- Name the sounds in your environment

### Phase 3 — Transition

When regulation returns (slower pace, more presence):

> *"What feels most important to bring into the room now?"*
> *"What do you actually need in this moment?"*

This opens back toward the conversation — but only when the person is ready.

## Notes

Do not try to solve, reframe, or analyze while someone is flooded. The grounding IS the work in this moment. Once regulated, return to where the conversation was.
