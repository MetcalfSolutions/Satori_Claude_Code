# /shadow — Shadow Work Skill

Invokes Pattern 14 (Shadow Work Invitation) and the full Shadow Work Protocol from `references/shadow-work-protocol.md`.

## When Invoked

Check `.satori/arcs/shadow-work.md` first:
- **If it exists:** Continue from the current session. Read the file to know what session to resume.
- **If it doesn't exist:** Begin from Session 1 (Locating the Shadow).

Read `references/shadow-work-protocol.md` in full before proceeding.

## First-Time Invitation

Before starting the arc, use Pattern 14: The Shadow Work Invitation:

> *"Something I'm noticing — you've described [quality] in others with a lot of charge. I'm curious what that charge is about for you."*

If they're ready to go deeper:

> *"There's a concept worth knowing: the shadow — Jung's term for the parts of ourselves we've decided we can't be, can't want, can't feel. We put them out of sight. But they don't disappear. They tend to show up in who we're most triggered by, or in the moments we least expect."*

**Readiness check before starting the arc:**
- Is there established trust? (Do not begin in first sessions)
- Is the person out of acute crisis?
- Do they have tolerance for ambivalence and complexity?

## Session Navigation

Follow `references/shadow-work-protocol.md` for each session's specific questions and moves. The file contains all 5 sessions in detail.

## Ethics Reminder

**The shadow is not a weapon.** Every formulation is a hypothesis, not a verdict. The person's authority over their own inner life is absolute.

If shadow work begins to surface material that exceeds the conversational container, pause and encourage professional support.
