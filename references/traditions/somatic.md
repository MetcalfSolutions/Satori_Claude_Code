## Core Concepts
- **The Body Keeps the Score** — Bessel van der Kolk: trauma is stored in the body as incomplete defensive responses. The body remembers what the mind has dissociated.
- **Somatic Experiencing (Peter Levine)** — Trauma healing through completing interrupted defensive responses. Titrated approach to body sensations. *Waking the Tiger*.
- **Felt Sense (Eugene Gendlin)** — The body's holistic, pre-verbal knowing. Focusing: attending to the felt sense of a situation rather than thinking about it.
- **Interoception** — Sensing the body's internal state. The foundation of emotional awareness. Many people have learned to ignore this signal.
- **Pendulation** — Moving between sensation and resource, between activation and settling. Building capacity through oscillation.
- **Grounding** — Using the body's contact with the physical environment (gravity, breath, touch) to return to the present moment.
- **Embodied Emotion** — Emotions are not "in the mind" — they have texture, location, temperature, and movement in the body.

## Key Figures
- **Bessel van der Kolk** — *The Body Keeps the Score*. The most widely read book on trauma.
- **Peter Levine** — *Waking the Tiger*, *In an Unspoken Voice*. Somatic Experiencing.
- **Eugene Gendlin** — *Focusing*. The felt sense and how to work with it.
- **Gabor Maté** — *The Myth of Normal*. How the body expresses unprocessed experience.

## When to Draw On This
- Embodied distress without clear cognitive explanation
- "I know this intellectually but it doesn't help"
- Trauma held in the body, not yet accessible to narrative
- When conversation has been too cognitive for too long (use the Somatic Check)
- Recurring physical symptoms with no clear medical cause

## The Somatic Check (Pattern)
> *"I want to pause the thinking for a second. What are you noticing in your body right now?"*
This is one of the most useful moves in a conversation that has gone too cognitive.
