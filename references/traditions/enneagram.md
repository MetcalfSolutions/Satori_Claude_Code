## Core Concepts
- **Nine Types** — Nine patterns of attention, motivation, and defense. Each type has a core fear, core desire, and automatic strategy.
- **Three Centers of Intelligence:**
  - **Head (5, 6, 7)** — Fear is the underlying emotion. Core question: is the world safe?
  - **Heart (2, 3, 4)** — Shame is the underlying emotion. Core question: am I loveable/worthy?
  - **Gut (8, 9, 1)** — Anger is the underlying emotion. Core question: do I have the right to exist?
- **Wings** — Influence from adjacent types shapes the expression of the core type.
- **Lines (Arrows)** — Movement under stress (integration/disintegration arrows).
- **Levels of Development** — Within each type, healthy to unhealthy functioning. The same type can look completely different at different levels.
- **Three Subtypes** — Self-preservation, social, and one-to-one. The subtypes can look so different they obscure the type.
- **The Passion** — Each type's emotional pattern. The automatic, unconscious stance that shapes all behavior.

## Key Figures
- **Don Richard Riso & Russ Hudson** — *The Wisdom of the Enneagram*. The most comprehensive resource.
- **Beatrice Chestnut** — *The Complete Enneagram*. Especially good on the three subtypes.
- **Ian Morgan Cron & Suzanne Stabile** — *The Road Back to You*. The most accessible introduction.
- **Richard Rohr** — *The Enneagram: A Christian Perspective*.

## When to Draw On This
- Someone exploring the deep structure of their recurring patterns
- Interpersonal conflicts that repeat regardless of the specific people involved
- Questions of deep motivation — why the same pattern keeps showing up
- Someone already working with Enneagram who wants deeper engagement
