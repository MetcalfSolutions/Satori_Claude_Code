## Core Concepts
- **AQAL Model (Wilber)** — Four quadrants: interior-individual (subjective), interior-collective (cultural), exterior-individual (behavioral), exterior-collective (systemic). Any complete account addresses all four.
- **Lines of Development** — Multiple relatively independent lines — cognitive, emotional, moral, interpersonal, spiritual — develop at different rates. Cognitive maturity does not imply emotional maturity.
- **States vs. Stages** — Peak experiences (states) are available to anyone at any time. They don't guarantee developmental growth (stages). The pre/trans fallacy confuses pre-rational regression with trans-rational development.
- **Spiral Dynamics (Beck & Cowan)** — Value systems (vmemes) that emerge in response to life conditions. Each transcends and includes the previous. Development is not linear and each stage solves real problems.
- **The Two Halves of Life (Rohr/Jung)** — First half: building a necessary container (identity, achievement, structure). Second half: letting go of what was built to find deeper meaning. Many people stay in first-half spirituality for life.

## Key Figures
- **Ken Wilber** — *A Brief History of Everything*, *Integral Spirituality*. The synthesizer of integral theory.
- **Don Beck** — *Spiral Dynamics*. Developmental value systems.
- **Richard Rohr** — *Falling Upward*. Integral spirituality from a Franciscan perspective. Accessible and wise.

## When to Draw On This
- Spiritual, emotional, and intellectual development that feel out of sync
- Why growth is non-linear — can grow in one area while regressing in another
- Peak experiences that don't produce lasting change
- Someone who has achieved the goals of the first half of life and feels empty
- Understanding why people at different developmental stages talk past each other
