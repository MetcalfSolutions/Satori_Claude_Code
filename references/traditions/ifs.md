# IFS (Internal Family Systems) — Deep Reference

**Selection guide:** `traditions-quickref.md` | **Load when:** deeper depth needed on IFS

---

## Core Concepts
- **Parts** — The mind contains many semi-autonomous parts, each with its own perspective, feeling, and intention.
- **Exiles** — Parts carrying pain, shame, fear, or grief from the past. Often young. Pushed out of awareness because their feelings are overwhelming.
- **Managers** — Protective parts that try to prevent exiles from being triggered. Control, planning, perfectionism, withdrawal.
- **Firefighters** — Reactive protectors that activate when exiles are triggered anyway. Impulsivity, addiction, dissociation, self-harm, rage.
- **The Self** — The core of the person: curious, compassionate, calm, confident, creative, courageous, connected, clear. Not a part but the ground from which parts can be witnessed. The Self cannot be damaged — only obscured.
- **Unblending** — Creating some separation between the Self and an activated part so the Self can be present to it without being it.
- **Trailheads** — Entry points into the inner system, often triggered by present-day reactions.

## Key Figures
- **Richard Schwartz** — The founder of IFS. *No Bad Parts* (accessible), *Internal Family Systems Therapy* (clinical).

## Key Texts
- *No Bad Parts* (Schwartz) — Accessible, warm, excellent introduction.
- *Self-Therapy* (Jay Earley) — Practical workbook for independent use.

## When to Draw On This
- Inner conflict, ambivalence, or "I don't know why I keep doing this"
- A protective behavior that makes sense as protection, even when it causes harm
- Someone who feels pulled in multiple directions by competing inner voices
- Shame cycles — the exile and the manager working against each other
- When parts language emerges naturally in the person's speech ("part of me wants X but...")

## Usage Notes
Use parts language naturally, not as a labeled framework. Say "there's a part of you that..." rather than "in IFS terms, this would be your manager." The framework is invisible; the recognition is what matters.

## Sample Language
> *"Part of you knows exactly what needs to happen — and another part is doing everything it can to make sure it doesn't. That's not weakness. That's protection. The question is what that second part is protecting."*
