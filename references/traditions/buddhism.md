## Core Concepts
- **Dukkha** — Suffering or unsatisfactoriness. Clinging to impermanent things causes suffering.
- **Impermanence (Anicca)** — All conditioned things arise and pass away. Liberation, not despair.
- **Non-Self (Anatta)** — The self is not a fixed entity but a process, a flowing pattern.
- **The Four Noble Truths** — Suffering exists; it arises from craving; it can cease; the Eightfold Path leads there.
- **Mindfulness (Sati)** — Present-moment awareness without judgment. A quality of relationship to experience.
- **Compassion (Karuna) & Loving-Kindness (Metta)** — Extending care to oneself and all others.
- **The Middle Way** — Between extremes. Moderation as wisdom.
- **Beginner's Mind (Shoshin)** — Approaching experience with openness, as if for the first time.
- **Interdependence (Interbeing)** — Nothing exists in isolation; all phenomena arise in relationship.
- **The Three Poisons** — Greed, hatred, and delusion — roots of most suffering.

## Key Figures
- **Thich Nhat Hanh** — Present-moment awareness, engaged Buddhism, *Interbeing*.
- **Pema Chödrön** — Groundlessness, fear, radical self-compassion.
- **Ajahn Chah** — Thai forest tradition. Clear, direct, tinged with humor.
- **Tara Brach** — RAIN practice. Self-compassion and difficult emotions.
- **Sharon Salzberg** — Metta specialist. *Lovingkindness*.

## Key Texts
- *When Things Fall Apart* (Pema Chödrön)
- *The Miracle of Mindfulness* (Thich Nhat Hanh)
- *A Path with Heart* (Kornfield)
- *Radical Acceptance* (Tara Brach)
- *Dhammapada* — "Mind is the forerunner of all actions."

## When to Draw On This
- Attachment to an outcome, a person, an identity, a past
- Resisting the reality of change or loss
- Needing grounding in the present moment
- Self-criticism or shame
- Grief, transition, or the ending of anything significant
- The difference between pain (inevitable) and suffering (what we add to it)
