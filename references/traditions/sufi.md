# Sufi / Islamic Wisdom — Deep Reference

**Selection guide:** `traditions-quickref.md` | **Load when:** deeper depth needed on Sufi/Islamic wisdom
**Cultural care:** Sufi wisdom is inseparable from its Islamic context. Not a standalone "mystical tradition."

---

## Core Concepts
- **The Nafs (النفس)** — The ego-self in stages: commanding (base impulses) → self-accusing (aware of failings) → tranquil (at peace). Growth is refinement, not elimination.
- **Dhikr (ذكر)** — Remembrance; repeated invocation that functions as presence practice.
- **The Heart (Qalb)** — Seat of spiritual intelligence. Must be polished — like a mirror — to reflect reality clearly.
- **Fana (فناء)** — Ego-dissolution; the self becomes transparent enough for something larger to shine through.
- **Tawakkul (توكل)** — Radical trust; acting fully while releasing attachment to outcome.
- **Divine Love (Ishq)** — Love as the fundamental force of spiritual transformation. The lover and the Beloved are ultimately not-two.

## Key Figures
- **Rumi** — The poet of divine love. The *Masnavi* is called "the Quran in Persian." Wrote from devastating loss and its transformation.
- **Hafiz** — Persian poet of ecstatic divine love, humor, and wine as spiritual metaphor.
- **Ibn Arabi** — Unity of Being (*Wahdat al-Wujud*). Most philosophically rigorous Sufi thinker.
- **Al-Ghazali** — *Revival of the Religious Sciences*. The theologian who turned from academic brilliance to lived spiritual experience.
- **Rabia al-Adawiyya** — First great female Sufi mystic. Selfless divine love.

## Key Texts
- *Masnavi* (Rumi) — Six books of spiritual poetry.
- *The Conference of the Birds* (Attar) — Allegorical journey of the soul.

## When to Draw On This
- Yearning for transcendence that cannot be satisfied by ordinary means
- Wrestling with surrender vs. control
- Love as a spiritual path — not sentimental but as furnace of transformation
- The purification of ego when the self's strategies are no longer working
- Someone from a Muslim or Sufi background whose language and devotional framework deserves honor
