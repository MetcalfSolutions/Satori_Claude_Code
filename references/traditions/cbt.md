# CBT (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy) — Deep Reference

**Selection guide:** `traditions-quickref.md` | **Load when:** deeper depth needed on CBT

## Core Concepts
- **The Cognitive Triangle** — Thoughts, feelings, and behaviors are interconnected. Changing any one affects the others.
- **Cognitive Distortions** — Systematic errors in thinking that produce unnecessary suffering:
  - *Catastrophizing* — Assuming the worst will happen
  - *All-or-nothing thinking* — No middle ground
  - *Mind reading* — Assuming you know what others think
  - *Emotional reasoning* — "I feel it, therefore it's true"
  - *Should statements* — Rigid rules that produce shame and resentment
  - *Personalization* — Taking responsibility for things outside your control
  - *Overgeneralization* — "This always happens to me"
  - *Discounting the positive* — Dismissing good evidence
- **Thought Records** — Structured practice of identifying the situation, automatic thought, emotion, evidence for and against, and a more balanced thought.
- **Behavioral Activation** — When depression reduces activity, deliberately increasing engagement with valued activities restores mood through action rather than waiting to feel motivated.
- **Exposure** — Gradually confronting feared situations reduces avoidance-maintained anxiety.

## Key Figures
- **Aaron Beck** — The founder of CBT. Originally developed for depression.
- **Albert Ellis** — REBT (Rational Emotive Behavior Therapy). More confrontational.
- **David Burns** — *Feeling Good*. The most accessible popularization of CBT.

## Key Texts
- *Feeling Good* (David Burns) — The classic accessible introduction.
- *Mind Over Mood* (Greenberger & Padesky) — The best workbook.

## When to Draw On This
- Identifiable cognitive loops: catastrophizing, all-or-nothing, mind reading
- Persistent anxiety with identifiable thought patterns
- Someone who benefits from a structured, practical frame
- When insight has been gained but behavior hasn't changed — behavioral experiments help

## Note
CBT is a starting point, not a ceiling. For deeper material (early patterns, relational origins, embodied distress), layer with Schema Therapy, IFS, or somatic frameworks.
