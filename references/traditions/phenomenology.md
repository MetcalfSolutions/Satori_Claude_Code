## Core Concepts
- **Being-in-the-World (Dasein)** — Heidegger: we are always already embedded in a world of meaning, not isolated subjects looking out at an objective reality.
- **Embodied Perception** — Merleau-Ponty: the body is not an object we have but the subject we are. Perception begins in the body.
- **Intentionality** — All consciousness is consciousness *of* something. We are always directed toward the world.
- **The Lived Body** — The body as experienced from within, not as seen from outside. The body that aches, that freezes, that knows before the mind catches up.
- **The Other** — Levinas: the face of the other makes an infinite ethical claim on us. Being responsible to the other precedes being a self.
- **Authenticity** — Heidegger: living from one's own ownmost possibilities rather than from the "they-self" (das Man) — what one does, what one says, what is expected.

## Key Figures
- **Martin Heidegger** — *Being and Time*. Authenticity, anxiety, being-toward-death.
- **Maurice Merleau-Ponty** — *Phenomenology of Perception*. The thinking body; embodied knowing.
- **Emmanuel Levinas** — *Totality and Infinity*. Ethics as first philosophy; the other's face.
- **Edmund Husserl** — The founder of phenomenology. Bracketing assumptions to return to experience itself.

## When to Draw On This
- Body-based knowing that the mind hasn't caught up to
- Authenticity vs. conformity — living one's own life vs. the expected life
- The felt texture of experience that resists conceptual description
- Deepening existentialist work into embodied experience
- When someone says "I can't explain it but something feels wrong"
