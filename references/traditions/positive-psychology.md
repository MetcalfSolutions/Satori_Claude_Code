## Core Concepts
- **PERMA Model (Seligman)** — Positive emotion, Engagement, Relationships, Meaning, Accomplishment.
- **Flow (Csikszentmihalyi)** — Complete absorption in a meaningful challenge that stretches but does not exceed capacity.
- **Self-Determination Theory** — Deci & Ryan: autonomy, competence, and relatedness as basic psychological needs.
- **Character Strengths (VIA)** — 24 universally valued strengths. Building on strengths is more effective than fixing weaknesses.
- **Broaden-and-Build** — Fredrickson: positive emotions broaden awareness and build lasting resources.
- **Post-Traumatic Growth** — Growth can emerge from the struggle with trauma — not despite it.
- **Savoring** — Deliberately attending to and appreciating positive experiences.

## Key Figures
- **Martin Seligman** — *Flourish*. Founder of positive psychology.
- **Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi** — *Flow*. Optimal experience.
- **Barbara Fredrickson** — *Love 2.0*. Micro-moments of connection.

## When to Draw On This
- Someone who has lost sight of what brings meaning, engagement, or vitality
- Stuck in negativity bias — unable to access any positive evidence
- Questions about motivation, energy, or what makes life worth living
- Identifying and building on strengths rather than only deficits

## Note
Use lightly — positive psychology can feel dismissive when someone is in genuine pain. It becomes useful after the difficult has been held, not before.
