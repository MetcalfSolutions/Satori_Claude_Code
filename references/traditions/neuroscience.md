## Core Concepts
- **Neuroplasticity** — The brain changes in response to experience throughout life. What we practice, we become. This is genuinely hopeful: patterns are not fixed.
- **The Default Mode Network (DMN)** — Active during mind-wandering, rumination, self-referential thought. Meditation reduces DMN dominance. Rumination is the DMN without regulation.
- **Predictive Processing** — The brain actively predicts experience, then updates models when predictions fail. We perceive what we expect. Changing models changes perception.
- **Constructed Emotion (Lisa Feldman Barrett)** — Emotions are not hardwired universal responses but actively constructed by the brain from body signals, context, and past experience. Genuinely liberating: emotions can be recontextualized.
- **Interoception** — The brain's sensing of the body's internal state. Foundation of emotional awareness and regulation.
- **Negativity Bias** — The brain attends to threats more than rewards. Evolved for survival, costly in modern life. Requires deliberate effort to counter.
- **The Social Brain** — The brain is fundamentally social, optimized for connection. Loneliness is physiologically harmful. Co-regulation is not a luxury.

## Key Figures
- **Lisa Feldman Barrett** — *How Emotions Are Made*. Emotions as construction, not detection.
- **Antonio Damasio** — *Descartes' Error*. Inseparability of emotion and reason. Somatic markers.
- **Daniel Siegel** — Interpersonal neurobiology. *Mindsight*, *The Developing Mind*.
- **Rick Hanson** — *Hardwiring Happiness*. Deliberate use of neuroplasticity.
- **Iain McGilchrist** — *The Master and His Emissary*. Left vs. right hemisphere and the impoverishment of purely analytical culture.

## When to Draw On This
- Why patterns feel automatic — neuroplasticity offers genuine hope for change
- Confusing or overwhelming emotions — constructed emotion is empowering
- Rumination — the DMN without regulation
- Why knowing something doesn't automatically change behavior
- To offer hope that change is neurologically real, not just aspirational
