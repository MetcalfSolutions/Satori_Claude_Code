## Worden's Four Tasks of Mourning
1. **Accept the reality of the loss** — Not just intellectually but emotionally and bodily.
2. **Process the pain of grief** — Actively engaging with the pain. Suppression prolongs grief; engagement moves through it.
3. **Adjust to a world without the deceased** — External (practical), internal (who am I now?), and spiritual (making sense within one's meaning-framework).
4. **Find an enduring connection while embarking on a new life** — The relationship continues, transformed.

These are tasks, not stages. They can be worked in any order.

## Continuing Bonds Theory (Klass, Silverman, Nickman)
- Grief does not end with "letting go." Healthy mourning maintains a *transformed* relationship with what was lost.
- Ongoing connection to a deceased person (talking to them, feeling their presence, carrying their values) is not pathology — it is healthy mourning.
- The relationship continues in a different form.

## Dual Process Model (Stroebe & Schut)
- Grief oscillates between:
  - **Loss-oriented coping** — Processing the loss itself (crying, yearning, remembering)
  - **Restoration-oriented coping** — Rebuilding daily life around the loss
- The oscillation IS the process. Moving between grief and getting on with life is not inconsistency — it is how human beings grieve.

## Ambiguous Loss (Pauline Boss)
- **Physical absence, psychological presence** — Missing persons, estrangement, immigration, deployment.
- **Physical presence, psychological absence** — Dementia, addiction, severe mental illness.
- Clean grief is not possible when the loss won't resolve. Finding meaning without closure is the work.

## When to Draw On This
- Bereavement of any kind
- Ambiguous losses (divorce, estrangement, illness)
- Someone being told they should be "over it" by now
- Survivor's guilt, complicated grief
- When someone feels their ongoing relationship with a deceased person is shameful or crazy

## Key Texts
- *On Grief and Grieving* (Kübler-Ross & Kessler) — Moving beyond the stages model
- *Finding Meaning* (David Kessler) — A sixth stage
- *Ambiguous Loss* (Pauline Boss)
- *The Year of Magical Thinking* (Joan Didion) — Literary testimony
