## Core Concepts
- **Early Maladaptive Schemas (EMS)** — Deep, pervasive patterns developed in childhood through unmet core emotional needs. Self-perpetuating — they filter experience to confirm themselves.
- **Schema Domains:**
  - *Disconnection/Rejection:* Abandonment, Mistrust/Abuse, Emotional Deprivation, Defectiveness/Shame, Social Isolation
  - *Impaired Autonomy:* Dependence/Incompetence, Vulnerability, Enmeshment, Failure
  - *Impaired Limits:* Entitlement, Insufficient Self-Control
  - *Other-Directedness:* Subjugation, Self-Sacrifice, Approval-Seeking
  - *Overvigilance/Inhibition:* Negativity, Emotional Inhibition, Unrelenting Standards, Punitiveness
- **Schema Modes** — Moment-to-moment states: Vulnerable Child (hurt, scared), Angry Child (frustrated), Detached Protector (numb, withdrawn), Punitive Parent (inner critic), Healthy Adult (integrated, compassionate).
- **Coping Styles** — Surrender (acting as if the schema is true), Avoidance (escaping the feeling), Overcompensation (doing the opposite).
- **Schema Chemistry** — Schemas attract complementary schemas in relationships. Someone with Self-Sacrifice is drawn to someone with Entitlement. The fit feels natural because it replicates the familiar.

## Key Figures
- **Jeffrey Young** — The founder of Schema Therapy.
- **Janet Klosko** — *Reinventing Your Life* (with Young). Most accessible non-clinical introduction.

## When to Draw On This
- Deeply entrenched relational patterns that repeat across different relationships
- Present-day reactions that seem disproportionate to the current trigger
- Someone who has done CBT but feels it doesn't reach the deeper material
- Relationship patterns where both partners seem locked in roles they can't exit

## Sample Language
> *"Something about this reaction feels bigger than the current situation — like it's connecting to something older. Does that resonate?"*
> *"There's a pattern here that might have roots earlier than this relationship — a kind of template for how you expect people to respond. It was probably accurate once."*
