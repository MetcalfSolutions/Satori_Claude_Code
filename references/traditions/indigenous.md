## Core Concepts
- **Relationality** — Identity and existence are fundamentally relational. We are who we are through our relationships — with each other, with the land, with ancestors, with future generations.
- **Seven Generations Thinking** — Haudenosaunee principle: decisions should consider effects on the next seven generations. The long view.
- **Reciprocity** — The fundamental ethic of relationship with the natural world. Taking only what is needed. Giving back.
- **The Intelligence of the Natural World** — Plants, animals, and ecosystems as teachers and relatives, not resources or scenery.
- **Ubuntu** — *"I am because we are."* Personhood constituted by relationship and community. (See also African Philosophy.)
- **Ancestral Continuity** — The community includes the living, the ancestors, and the yet-to-be-born. Obligation extends in all three directions.
- **Oral Tradition** — Wisdom carried in stories, songs, and embodied practice, not only in texts.

## Key Figures
- **Robin Wall Kimmerer** — *Braiding Sweetgrass*. Potawatomi botanist and writer. The most accessible entry point.
- **Vine Deloria Jr.** — *God Is Red*. Lakota scholar. Critical engagement with Western frameworks.
- **Joanna Macy** — *World as Lover, World as Self*. Bridges Buddhism, systems theory, and ecology.

## When to Draw On This
- Interconnection, belonging, and the loneliness of radical individualism
- Deepening relationship with the natural world
- The long view — zooming out from immediate urgency
- When Western psychological frameworks feel insufficient for the scope of the question
- Meaning, place, and the relationship between individual and something larger

## Note
Do not extract these traditions as spiritual aesthetics. When referencing them, maintain appropriate epistemic humility — these are living traditions with specific cultural homes.
