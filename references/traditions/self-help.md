## Core Frameworks

### Habit Formation (James Clear — Atomic Habits)
- **Identity-Based Change** — Behavior follows identity. "I am a person who..." is more durable than outcome goals.
- **The Four Laws of Behavior Change** — Make it obvious, attractive, easy, satisfying.
- **The 1% Rule** — Small improvements compound. 1% better each day.
- **Environment Design** — Design your environment to make desired behaviors the default.
- **Habit Stacking** — Attach new habits to existing ones.

### Tiny Habits (BJ Fogg)
- Start smaller than feels useful. The emotion after a behavior determines if it sticks.
- **B = MAP** — Behavior = Motivation × Ability × Prompt. Ability is often the bottleneck.

### Identity and Self-Concept Change
- The gap between who someone is and who they want to be is bridged by *voting* for the new identity through small acts.
- Every action is a vote for the type of person you wish to become.

### The Motivation Myth
- Action precedes motivation. Don't wait to feel motivated. Start; motivation follows.

## When to Draw On This
- Practical behavior change after direction is clear
- Someone ready to move from insight to implementation
- Building consistency in a new practice or routine
- When abstract goals need to be made concrete and environmental

## Critical Note
Tactics without values are empty. Use these only after deeper work has established direction and meaning. Starting with tactics before identity and values are clear produces temporary change at best.
