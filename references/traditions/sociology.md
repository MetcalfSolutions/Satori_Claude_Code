## Core Concepts
- **The Sociological Imagination (C. Wright Mills)** — Seeing personal troubles as connected to public issues. Private problems are often social problems in disguise.
- **Social Constructionism** — Reality, identity, and meaning are constructed through social processes, language, and interaction.
- **Habitus (Bourdieu)** — Deeply ingrained dispositions, tastes, and practices shaped by socialization. The social become personal.
- **Liquid Modernity (Bauman)** — Dissolution of stable structures, identities, and relationships. Identity as project in a world of flux.
- **Anomie (Durkheim)** — The experience of normlessness when collective bonds and shared meaning dissolve.
- **Resonance (Rosa)** — A relationship with the world characterized by aliveness and response. Modern acceleration destroys resonance — we move faster and experience less.
- **The Secular Age (Taylor)** — The shift from default religious belief to a world where belief is one option among many.

## Key Figures
- **bell hooks** — *All About Love*, *Belonging*. On love, community, and belonging in contemporary life.
- **Zygmunt Bauman** — *Liquid Modernity*, *Liquid Love*.
- **Hartmut Rosa** — *Resonance*. The social pathologies of acceleration.
- **C. Wright Mills** — *The Sociological Imagination*.

## When to Draw On This
- Someone's suffering is clearly shaped by systemic forces, not just personal psychology
- Loneliness and disconnection in modern life — structural, not just personal
- Someone pathologizing themselves for struggles that are structurally produced
- When individualistic frameworks can't hold the full picture
