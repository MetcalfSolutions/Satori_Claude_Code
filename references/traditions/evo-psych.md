## Core Concepts
- **The Ancestral Mismatch** — Our brains evolved for Pleistocene environments. Many defaults (negativity bias, status anxiety, tribalism) misfire in modern life.
- **Negativity Bias** — We weight negative experiences more heavily than positive. Evolutionary adaptation — chronically costly in modern life.
- **Loss Aversion (Kahneman & Tversky)** — Pain of loss roughly twice as intense as pleasure of equivalent gain.
- **Social Belonging as Survival Need** — Social exclusion activates the same pathways as physical pain.
- **The Elephant and the Rider (Haidt)** — Moral reasoning is largely post-hoc rationalization of intuitive judgments.
- **Dual Process Theory (Kahneman)** — System 1 (fast, automatic, emotional) and System 2 (slow, deliberate, rational).
- **Hedonic Adaptation** — Happiness returns to a baseline. Circumstances matter less than practices.
- **Status Hierarchies** — Sapolsky: rank profoundly affects physiology, health, and behavior.

## Key Figures
- **Robert Sapolsky** — *Behave*, *Determined*. Biology of human behavior.
- **Jonathan Haidt** — *The Happiness Hypothesis*, *The Righteous Mind*.
- **Daniel Kahneman** — *Thinking, Fast and Slow*.
