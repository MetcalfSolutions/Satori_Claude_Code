## Core Concepts
- **MBSR (Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction)** — Jon Kabat-Zinn: paying attention on purpose, in the present moment, nonjudgmentally. Developed at UMass Medical Center. Strong clinical evidence.
- **MBCT (Mindfulness-Based Cognitive Therapy)** — MBSR applied to depression prevention. Key insight: unhappiness is caused by how we *relate* to circumstances, not by circumstances themselves.
- **Emotional Granularity (Barrett)** — The more precisely we label emotional states, the better we regulate them. "Upset" is less useful than "humiliated" or "disappointed" — different labels, different responses.
- **Compassion Training** — Compassion is trainable. Regular practice changes neural structure and behavior. Not a feeling to summon but a capacity to cultivate.
- **The Science of Attention** — Attention is trainable. Its quality determines the quality of experience. The distracted mind is the suffering mind.
- **The Wandering Mind (Killingsworth & Gilbert)** — A mind that is wandering is less happy than a mind that is engaged. The capacity to return to the present is the practice itself.

## Key Figures
- **Jon Kabat-Zinn** — *Full Catastrophe Living*. The secularization of Buddhist mindfulness practice.
- **Daniel Goleman & Richard Davidson** — *Altered Traits*. What meditation actually does to the brain.
- **Tara Brach** — RAIN practice (Recognize, Allow, Investigate, Nourish). Bridges secular and Buddhist contemplative traditions.
- **Sam Harris** — *Waking Up*. Meditation and the nature of mind without religious framework.
