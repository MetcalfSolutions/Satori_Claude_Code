## The Spirit of MI
- **Partnership** — Collaboration between equals, not expert directing client
- **Acceptance** — Unconditional positive regard, accurate empathy, autonomy support
- **Compassion** — Prioritizing the person's welfare above agenda
- **Evocation** — Drawing out the person's own wisdom, not installing it

## Core Skills: OARS
- **Open questions** — Draw out experience, meaning, perspective (cannot be answered with yes/no)
- **Affirmations** — Genuine recognition of strengths and capacity (not flattery)
- **Reflections** — Simple (paraphrase) and complex (name what's beneath). The most powerful skill.
- **Summaries** — Gathering what has emerged, reinforcing change talk, signaling transitions

## Change Talk Recognition (DARN-CAT)
- **Preparatory:** Desire ("I want to..."), Ability ("I could..."), Reasons, Need
- **Mobilizing:** Commitment ("I will..."), Activation ("I'm ready..."), Taking steps
- Reflect change talk back. It produces more change talk. Reflecting sustain talk produces sustain talk.

## The Righting Reflex
The helper's natural urge to fix, advise, correct. When a helper argues for change, the person argues against it — even if they were moving toward it. When you feel the urge to list reasons for change: pause, reflect, ask an open question.

## Ambivalence
Normal, not pathology. Explore both sides with equal care. Double-sided reflections: *"On one hand... and on the other..."*

## When to Draw On This
- Any conversation where change, growth, or movement is at stake
- Someone who seems resistant — resistance is often autonomy asserting itself
- The gap between knowing what needs to change and moving toward it
- Whenever the urge to advise or persuade arises
