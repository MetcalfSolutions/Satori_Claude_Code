## Core Concepts (McAdams)
- **The Life Story** — Identity is a self-constructed, internalized narrative that integrates the reconstructed past, perceived present, and anticipated future.
- **Chapters** — Life story divided into distinct periods. Transitions between chapters often reveal the deepest self-understanding.
- **High Points, Low Points, Turning Points** — Key scenes that define the story. Emotional texture reveals narrative themes.
- **Redemption Sequences** — Bad scenes lead to good outcomes. Associated with wellbeing, generativity, positive life satisfaction.
- **Contamination Sequences** — Good scenes turn bad. Associated with depression, stagnation, psychological distress.
- **Autobiographical Reasoning** — The active process of making meaning from life events. What the person concludes about themselves from their story.
- **Narrative Coherence** — How well the life story hangs together. Gaps, contradictions, and silences are as telling as what is said.

## Narrative Therapy (White & Epston)
- **Externalizing Problems** — The problem is the problem; the person is not the problem.
- **Dominant Narrative** — The story that organizes experience, often installed by others, often limiting.
- **Unique Outcomes** — Moments when the person acted contrary to the problem story — entry points for re-authoring.
- **Re-authoring** — Constructing a richer, more complex story that includes exceptions and alternatives.

## When to Draw On This
- Identity transition — life chapter ending, major change
- Someone who feels their story has broken down or doesn't make sense
- Finding coherence across past, present, and future
- Externalizing a problem that the person has merged with their identity
- Someone who needs to tell their story before they can think about it differently

## Key Prompts
> *"If you divided your life into chapters — what would they be? What would you title them?"*
> *"Has there been a moment when something changed not just in your circumstances but in yourself?"*
> *"What story are you telling yourself about why this happened?"*
