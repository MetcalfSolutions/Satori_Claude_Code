# African Philosophy & Ubuntu — Deep Reference

**Selection guide:** `traditions-quickref.md` | **Load when:** deeper depth needed on African philosophy
**Cultural care:** African philosophy is not a monolith. The continent contains thousands of distinct traditions. Engage as genuine philosophy, not spiritual aesthetic.

---

## Core Concepts
- **Ubuntu** — *"I am because we are."* Not a proverb but an ontological claim: personhood is constituted by relationship and community. A person is a person through other persons.
- **Ujamaa (Nyerere)** — Collective economics, cooperative living. The community's wellbeing is inseparable from the individual's.
- **Sankofa** — Akan concept: *"Go back and get it."* Understanding the past is necessary to move forward. The past is a resource you carry, not a burden behind you.
- **Communalism** — Individual flourishing requires communal health. The African philosophical critique of Western atomistic individualism.
- **Ancestral Continuity** — The community includes the living, the ancestors, and the yet-to-be-born. Obligation extends in all three directions.
- **Oral Tradition & Embodied Knowledge** — Wisdom carried in stories, proverbs, songs, and practice rather than only in texts.
- **Négritude (Senghor, Césaire)** — Assertion of Black African cultural identity and values as counter-narrative to colonial devaluation.

## Key Figures
- **Kwame Gyekye** — *An Essay on African Philosophical Thought*. Rigorous treatment of Akan thought.
- **Desmond Tutu** — Ubuntu applied to restorative justice. *No Future Without Forgiveness*.
- **Julius Nyerere** — Philosopher of African socialism.
- **Achille Mbembe** — *On the Postcolony*. Contemporary postcolonial thought.
- **Mogobe Ramose** — On Ubuntu as genuine philosophy, not cultural slogan.

## When to Draw On This
- Questions of community, belonging, and relational personhood
- The limits of radical individualism
- Ancestral connection and obligation
- Identity in diaspora
- When Western frameworks of autonomous selfhood are insufficient
