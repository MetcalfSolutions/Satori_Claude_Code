# DBT (Dialectical Behavior Therapy) — Deep Reference

**Selection guide:** `traditions-quickref.md` | **Load when:** deeper depth needed on DBT

---

## Core Concepts
- **Dialectics** — Holding two opposing truths simultaneously. The central dialectic: acceptance AND change. Not contradictions — both true at once.
- **Radical Acceptance** — Complete acknowledgment of reality as it is, without judgment. Not approval, not resignation. Refusing to add suffering to pain by fighting what has already happened.
- **Wise Mind** — The synthesis of emotion mind (hot, reactive, feeling-driven) and rational mind (cold, logical, fact-driven). Intuitive knowing that arises when both are held.
- **The Biosocial Model** — Emotional vulnerability (biological) meets an invalidating environment (one that dismisses or punishes emotional expression). The result is chronic dysregulation — not a character flaw.
- **Distress Tolerance** — Surviving crisis without making it worse. Getting through the moment.
- **Emotion Regulation** — Understanding the function of emotions; opposite action when emotion doesn't fit the facts.
- **Interpersonal Effectiveness** — Asking for what you need (DEAR MAN) while maintaining the relationship (GIVE) and self-respect (FAST).
- **Walking the Middle Path** — Replacing "but" with "and." Validating experience while also naming what needs to change.

## Key Figures
- **Marsha Linehan** — The creator of DBT. Developed it for chronically suicidal patients. *Building a Life Worth Living* is her memoir — remarkable for its honesty.

## When to Draw On This
- Intense emotional reactivity — emotions that feel overwhelming and disproportionate
- Oscillation between extremes (all good / all bad, idealization / devaluation)
- Crisis moments where the immediate need is to not make things worse
- Interpersonal conflicts where both assertiveness and relationship preservation matter

## Sample Language
> *"Two things can be true at the same time — you can accept that this is how things are right now, and still want them to be different. That's not contradictory. That's honest."*
> *"Right now the question might not be 'how do I fix this?' but 'how do I get through this moment without making it worse?'"*
> *"What would wise mind say here — not the panicked part, not the rational part that wants to shut down the feeling, but the knowing that comes from both?"*
