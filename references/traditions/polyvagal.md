## Core Concepts
- **The Autonomic Nervous System** — Operates below conscious control. Continuously scans for safety and threat (neuroception).
- **Three States (Stephen Porges):**
  1. **Ventral Vagal** — Safe, connected, socially engaged. Open to learning, play, intimacy. The foundation of wellbeing.
  2. **Sympathetic** — Mobilized for fight or flight. Heart rate elevated, senses heightened, threat detected.
  3. **Dorsal Vagal** — Freeze, collapse, shutdown. Evolutionary last resort. Dissociation, immobility, numbness.
- **Neuroception** — Automatic, subcortical scanning of the environment for safety cues. Occurs before conscious awareness.
- **Co-regulation** — The nervous system regulates through contact with regulated nervous systems. Safety is communicated prosodically (voice), facially, and through presence. Co-regulation precedes self-regulation.
- **Window of Tolerance (Siegel)** — The zone of arousal within which the person can function effectively. Hyperarousal (sympathetic) and hypoarousal (dorsal vagal) are outside the window.
- **Titration** — Approaching difficult material gradually, in small doses, to avoid overwhelming the nervous system.
- **Glimmers** — Small moments that cue safety. The opposite of triggers.

## Key Figures
- **Stephen Porges** — The creator of Polyvagal Theory. *The Polyvagal Theory*, *The Pocket Guide to the Polyvagal Theory*.
- **Deb Dana** — *Anchored*. The most accessible application of polyvagal theory.

## When to Draw On This
- Physiological overwhelm, flooding, or shutdown
- Patterns of behavior beyond conscious control (avoidance, collapse, rage)
- Explaining why "just think differently" doesn't work when someone is in a threat state
- Understanding why safety in relationship is prerequisite to growth

## Note for Satori Use
When someone is flooded (racing speech, catastrophizing) or collapsed (flat, unreachable), regulation precedes reflection. Slow down, use shorter sentences, invite grounding. No frameworks until the nervous system returns to the window.
