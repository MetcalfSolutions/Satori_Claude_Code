## Decision-Making Models
- **Inversion** — Instead of "how do I succeed?", ask "how could I fail?" Identify what to avoid.
- **Second-Order Thinking** — Ask "and then what?" Consider downstream consequences, not just immediate effects.
- **Pre-Mortem** — Imagine the plan has failed. Work backward: what went wrong? Surfaces blind spots before commitment.
- **Regret Minimization (Bezos)** — Project forward to old age. Which choice would you regret not having made?
- **Expected Value** — Probability × magnitude. Avoids both overconfidence and excessive risk aversion.

## Clarity Models
- **First Principles Thinking** — Strip away assumptions and conventions; reason from fundamentals.
- **The Map Is Not the Territory** — Your model of reality is not reality. Useful but always incomplete.
- **Hanlon's Razor** — Never attribute to malice what can be adequately explained by incompetence (or miscommunication).
- **Occam's Razor** — Among competing explanations, prefer the simpler.
- **The Eisenhower Matrix** — Urgent/important grid. Urgent but not important is the trap.

## Behavior Change Models
- **Identity-Based Change (Clear)** — Behavior follows identity. "I am a person who..." is more durable than goals.
- **The 2-Minute Rule** — If a task takes less than 2 minutes, do it now.
- **Environment Design** — Make desired behaviors easier and undesired behaviors harder. Willpower is unreliable.

## When to Draw On This
- Someone with a concrete decision to make and no clear framework
- Someone stuck in analysis paralysis
- Practical behavior change after deeper work has established direction
- A simple, tangible tool when someone is tired of depth and needs traction

## Note
Tactics without values are empty. Use these after the deeper questions of direction and meaning have been at least partially addressed.
