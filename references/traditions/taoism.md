## Core Concepts
- **Tao (道)** — The Way; the underlying current of reality that cannot be named or grasped, only followed.
- **Wu Wei (无为)** — Non-action; effortless action in harmony with what is. Not passivity but the absence of forced striving. Like water, which shapes everything without effort.
- **Yin & Yang** — Complementary opposites that give rise to each other. Each contains the seed of the other.
- **Ziran (自然)** — Naturalness; being as you are without artifice.
- **The Uncarved Block (Pu)** — The undivided, original self before conditioning layers over it.
- **Te (德)** — Authentic virtue flowing from within when aligned with the Tao.
- **Reversal** — Things turn into their opposites. Softness overcomes hardness.

## Key Figures
- **Laozi** — Legendary author of the Tao Te Ching.
- **Zhuangzi** — More playful and paradoxical. The Cook and the Ox, the butterfly dream.

## Key Texts
- *Tao Te Ching* (Laozi) — Best translations: Stephen Mitchell, Ursula K. Le Guin.
- *Zhuangzi* — Burton Watson's translation is the standard.

## When to Draw On This
- Someone straining, forcing, or pushing against circumstances that won't yield
- Caught in the tyranny of doing, overthinking, or controlling
- Needs permission to stop, rest, let go, or let things unfold
- Questions of naturalness, authenticity, or living in alignment with one's nature
- The paradox of wanting control in situations that resist it
