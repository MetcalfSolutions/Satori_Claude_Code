## Core Concepts
- **Grace** — Unconditional love not earned by merit. Worth is given, not constructed.
- **Forgiveness** — Of self and others. One of the most psychologically potent ideas in any tradition.
- **Redemption** — Suffering and failure can be transformed; nothing is wasted.
- **The Kingdom Within** — Inner transformation as the site of spiritual life.
- **Love as Command and Ground** — The Great Commandment. The simplest and most demanding moral framework.
- **The Prodigal Son** — Return, welcome, and grace without condition.
- **Kenosis** — Self-emptying; the letting go of ego as spiritual path.
- **Dark Night of the Soul** — St. John of the Cross: spiritual desolation as necessary passage, not abandonment.

## Key Figures
- **C.S. Lewis** — *Mere Christianity*, *A Grief Observed* (his rawest work).
- **Thomas Merton** — Bridges Christianity and Eastern traditions. *New Seeds of Contemplation*.
- **Richard Rohr** — Integrates Christianity, psychology, Enneagram, mysticism. *Falling Upward*.
- **Henri Nouwen** — *The Return of the Prodigal Son*, *The Wounded Healer*. Deep, vulnerable.
- **Howard Thurman** — *Jesus and the Disinherited*. Spiritual life of those with their backs against the wall.

## Key Texts
- *The Sermon on the Mount* (Matthew 5–7)
- *Confessions* (Augustine) — Psychological and spiritual autobiography.
- *A Grief Observed* (C.S. Lewis) — Raw. Does not resolve neatly.
- *Falling Upward* (Richard Rohr) — Spirituality of the second half of life.

## When to Draw On This
- Shame, guilt, or unworthiness
- Inability to forgive self or another
- Meaning in suffering or loss
- Someone raised in a Christian context who needs this language honored
- Questions of love, worth, belonging
